# AI Writing Anti-Slop Guide

**MANDATORY: Read this file before writing any LinkedIn post. Write without using any words, tone, structure, or writing patterns listed below.**

## BANNED PATTERNS — Do Not Write Like This

### The Balanced Template
AI loves neat pairings. Don't do this:
- "Not only does X save time, but it also improves accuracy."
- "From automation to analytics, AI is reshaping business."
- "Whether you're a small business owner or a large enterprise..."
- "Clear, concise, and compelling."

### The Fake Suspense
- "But here's the catch..."
- "And the answer may surprise you..."
- "What happens next is interesting..."
- "Stay tuned until the end..."

### The Over-Polite
- "I hope this finds you well."
- "Thank you for reaching out."
- "I appreciate the opportunity..."
- "Please let me know if you have any questions."

### The Staccato Rhythm
Don't write fragmented one-liner paragraphs stacked on top of each other. Write in flowing paragraphs with real sentences that connect.

### Emotional Flattening
Don't keep the tone uniformly positive, polite, and safe. Have an edge. Be honest. Acknowledge what's hard.

## THE FORMULA TO USE

**Specific problem → Clear opinion → Concrete example → Practical point → Clean ending**

### Hooks That Work
- Start with a specific problem: "Most businesses do not need more tools. They need fewer manual steps."
- Start with a sharp opinion: "Your content does not sound human just because you added contractions."
- Start with a concrete scene: "You open LinkedIn and see the same post again: 'AI is changing everything.'"
- Start with a mistake: "The fastest way to sound like AI is to start with 'In today's fast-paced world.'"
- Start with a result: "I cut 40% from the draft and it immediately sounded more human."
- Start with contrast: "AI writes clean sentences. Humans write with pressure, memory, bias, and rhythm."

### Endings That Work
- End with the strongest point: "If the sentence could fit any topic, rewrite it."
- End with a consequence: "That is why generic writing disappears online."
- End with a challenge: "Before you publish, remove the sentence that sounds the most impressive. It is usually the weakest one."
- End with a practical action: "Cut the opener. Name the problem. Use one real example."
- End with a memorable line: "Human writing has fingerprints. AI writing has polish."

## SELF-CHECK BEFORE PUBLISHING

Ask these questions. If any answer is yes, rewrite:

1. Does it start too broad?
2. Does it use any banned words from the list above?
3. Does every paragraph feel perfectly balanced?
4. Does it explain the topic before making a point?
5. Could the same paragraph fit 20 other topics by swapping keywords?
6. Does every sentence have the same smooth rhythm?
7. Is the tone too safe, too polite, or too positive?
8. Is the conclusion just a summary with a generic CTA?
9. Would a real person say this sentence aloud?
10. Are the examples real, named, dated, measurable, or personal?

## NATURAL SENTENCE RHYTHM

AI rhythm is too smooth. Human rhythm is uneven.

**Use short sentences after long ones.**
Example: "Most AI drafts explain too much before saying anything useful. Cut the setup. Start where the tension is."

**Use fragments sometimes.**
Example: "Good idea. Weak opening. Too much polish."

**Use specific nouns.**
AI: "This solution improves operational efficiency."
Human: "This cuts billing errors and saves the receptionist two hours a day."

**Use contractions when natural.**
AI: "It is important to ensure that the message is clear."
Human: "Make the message clear."

**Use one honest limitation.**
AI: "This powerful tool transforms your workflow."
Human: "It helps with drafts, but you still need to check the facts."

**Break perfect symmetry.**
AI: "Clear, concise, and compelling."
Human: "Clear first. Compelling later."
